Topic Anomalies

This chart shows a time series of the anomalous topics for the selected period and for the given filters.
An anomalous topic is a topic for which the daily prevalence is significantly higher than the average daily prevalence for the same topic over the entire dataset.
The chart shows data from Social and Media data streams.

Usage

Data can be filtered by topics, sentiment and emotion values, and time period. When no filters are applied, all the topics are considered.

How to Interpret the Chart

The chart shows the daily prevalence of anomalous topics over time and it can be used to identify periods of increased anomalous topics.

Axis:

The y-axis represents the number of anomalous topics, and the x-axis represents time (day).

Colors:

Two colors are present, one for Social and one for Media stream (see chart's legend)

Tooltips:

Hovering over the chart will show the number and a list of anomalous topics for that day

Example
  • Period: 1st to 30th of April 2024
  • Filters: Climate topics, all sentiment and emotion values
