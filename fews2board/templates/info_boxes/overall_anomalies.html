This bar chart presents a comparative overview of topic anomalies, by data source (news and social media). The metric identifies any anomalies in topic prevalence, i.e. topics that are presenting a different attention than usual.

They are presented as the count of anomalous topics for each data stream for the selected period. By default, when no topic is filtered, the visualization displays a sum of topic anomalies for each data stream, per day.

For every topic, the average and standard deviation of the daily normalized prevalences are calculated considering the whole dataset; a topic is then tagged as anomalous on a specific date when its topic normalized prevalence is larger than the average + standard deviation.
